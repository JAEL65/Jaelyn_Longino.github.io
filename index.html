Research Abstract

The Impact of Model Resolution and Physics on Fixing the Central U.S. Dry Bias

Precipitation remains notoriously difficult to accurately simulate in Global Climate Models (GCMs), which long have suffered from a persistent summertime dry bias across the central United States. To address this structural issue, we evaluate the impact of atmospheric model resolution and cloud microphysics configurations within the NOAA Geophysical Fluid Dynamics Laboratory’s (GFDL) AM4 model forced by observed sea surface temperatures...
